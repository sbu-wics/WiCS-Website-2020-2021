Women in Computer Science

Stony Brook University

We are an organization whose mission is to provide encouragement and support to women, both graduate and undergraduate, in the field of computing. We aim to foster understanding between men and women in Computer Science and to improve women's involvement in the field. We hold events, both academic and social in nature, and work with other groups and the Computer Science department.

We are an official ACM-W chapter.

March WiCSter of the Month

Zhen Yi Pan

Please meet our March WiCSter of the month, Zhen Yi Pan!


Zhen Yi Pan is a second-year student pursuing a computer science major with a geology minor. She is part of the Stony Brook Robotics Team (SBRT), Women in Computer Science (WiCS) and Stony Brook Game Developers (SBGD). In addition to the extracurricular activities she is involved in, she is currently an undergraduate teaching assistant for CSE 220 (System Fundamentals I). She is also currently part of the Vertically Integrated Projects Program (VIP); she is on the Bioengineering Education, Application and Research (BEAR) team under Professor McKenna. She participates in many on-campus hackathons, such as SBUHacks and InnovateIT. She also attended WiCS annual Hackathon, and won the Best Nutrition Hack with her group Mehdad and Jahin with "Nutrinex". During her free time, she enjoys drawing casually, including pixel art.
