Women in Computer Science

Stony Brook University

Women in Computer Science (WiCS) at Stony Brook University is an organization whose mission is to provide encouragement and support to women in the field of computing. We aim to bridge the gap between men and women in Computer Science and to improve women's involvement in the field. We hold professional and social events, working with tech companies, other organizations, and the Computer Science department.

Anyone can join our club, regardless of major and gender.

We are an official ACM-W chapter.

April WiCSter of the Month

Chloe Mack

Hi WiCSters! We’re very excited to introduce our WiCSter of the month for April, Chloe Mack!!!

Chloe is a junior year Computer Science transfer student whose interest lies in NLP and Computer Vision. She is a student Veteran and a member of the VESO, currently running for a position on the EBoard. This semester she served as a Color Stack Sprout Fellowship squad leader. She will be interning this summer with a company in the defense industry. Chloe’s core values are Honor, Integrity and Personal Courage.

In her free time, Chloe works on launching her startup, personal development projects, reading, playing video games, and getting people interested in coding.

Favorite programming language: Python
Favorite movie: Hidden Figures
Favorite TV show: Black List
